Traits of the First Computers

  1. Large Size: Early computers were enormous, often taking up an entire room.
  2. Very Expensive: Cost millions of dollars to build and maintain.
  3. Slow Processing Speed: Could perform only a few thousand calculations per second.

History Highlights

  • The first general-purpose electronic computer in the USA was the ENIAC, invented around 1945–1946.
  • The Colossus in the UK was built to break German codes during WWII (1943–1944).
  • The first commercially available computer was the UNIVAC I, invented in 1951, used for business and government.
